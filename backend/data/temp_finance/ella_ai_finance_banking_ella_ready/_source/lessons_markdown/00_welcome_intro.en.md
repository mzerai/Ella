# Lesson 0 — Welcome to AI for Finance & Banking
## Artificial Intelligence Applied to Financial Services

### This course gets straight to the point

You work in a bank, an insurance company, a fintech, or a finance department. You hear about AI everywhere: credit scoring, fraud detection, KYC, chatbots, compliance, and automation.

But in finance, AI is never just a simple tool. It can influence a credit decision, block a transaction, guide a customer, analyze a sensitive file, or generate a compliance alert.

So you need to ask the right questions:

- Is it useful?
- Is it reliable?
- Is it explainable?
- Is it compliant?
- Is it acceptable for the customer and for the institution?

That is the purpose of this course.

---

### Why this course matters now

Financial institutions hold large volumes of data: transactions, repayment histories, KYC files, contracts, claims, supporting documents, internal reports, and customer conversations.

AI can help use this data to improve decisions, detect anomalies, reduce delays, and automate selected tasks.

But be careful: a high-performing model can create risk if it is opaque, biased, poorly controlled, or poorly documented.

In Tunisia, this topic is strategic. Banks and financial institutions already operate under strong expectations for governance, internal control, risk management, and compliance. BCT Circular No. 2021-05 defines a governance framework for Tunisian banks and financial institutions. [R1]

Personal data protection is also central. In Tunisia, Organic Law No. 2004-63 recognizes personal data protection as a fundamental right. [R2]

Internationally, the direction is similar. The NIST AI Risk Management Framework provides a structured approach to AI risk management around four functions: Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage. [R3]

---

### What you will learn

You will work on five key AI use cases in financial services.

**1. Credit scoring and risk analysis**  
You will understand how a model can help estimate the risk of a credit application. You will also learn why a score must remain explainable.

**2. Fraud detection**  
You will learn how to detect suspicious transactions, manage false positives, and choose metrics that fit financial risk.

**3. NLP for financial documents**  
You will use AI to analyze KYC documents, contracts, supporting documents, procedures, and compliance texts.

**4. Banking chatbots**  
You will design a customer assistant that gives useful answers without inventing facts, exposing sensitive data, or exceeding its role.

**5. Governance, compliance, and responsible AI**  
You will learn how to document an AI use case, identify risks, propose controls, and prepare a sheet for a risk or compliance committee.

---

### What you will be able to do

By the end of this course, you should be able to:

1. Identify a relevant AI use case in finance.
2. Choose an AI approach that fits the problem.
3. Read model results without being impressed by a single score.
4. Explain risks related to data, bias, explainability, and compliance.
5. Design a banking chatbot with guardrails.
6. Use NLP to analyze a document file.
7. Prepare an AI governance sheet for business, risk, or compliance teams.

The goal is not to turn you into a data scientist in four days.

The goal is more important: **you will learn how to guide, evaluate, and integrate AI in a real financial environment.**

---

### Watch this carefully

❌ **Common mistake**  
Judging banking AI only by accuracy or productivity gain.

✅ **Better approach**  
Evaluate each use case through three questions: performance, risk, and compliance.
